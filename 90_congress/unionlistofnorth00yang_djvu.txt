INTRODUCTION 


In  response  to  the  increasing  studies  of  North  Korean 
affairs,  and  the  increasing  need  for  publications  from  this  area, 

I  have  prepared  this  Union  List  of  issues  of  iJcf  serials  and  36 
newspapers  which  are  available  in  the  Library  of  Congress  (DLC) 
and  in  four  university  libraries:  Columbia  University  (symbol: 

NNC) ;  Harvard-University  (MH-HY) ;  the  University  of  California, 
Berkeley  (GU) ;  and  the  University  of  Washington,  Seattle  (WaU) . 

Included  also  are  86  serial  and  30  newspaper  titles 
found  in  published  sources  but  of  which  no  issues  have  been 
located  in  U.  S.  libraries.  These  titles,  inserted  in  alphabetical 

sequence,  are  distinguished  by  the  lack  of  holdings  statements.

2.  Newspapers  in  Korean,  English,  Russian,  Japanese  and  Chinese.

of  corporate  authors  and  titles  in  English;  2.  a  separate  list  of 
titles  available  from  each  library  (except  the  Library  of  Congress); 
and  3»  a  list  of  titles  in  Russian,  Japanese,  and  Chinese.

Entries  are  arranged  alphabetically  under  romanized  titles 
(in  the  case  of  the  Korean  language,  according  to  the  McCune-Reischauer 
system) ,  followed  by  both  the  vernacular  title  and  an  English  translation 
and  the  date  of  the  first  issue,  if  known. 

Pertinent  information  for  each  entry  is  supplied  in  the 
following  sequence:  publisher,  frequency,  name  of  organization 
sponsoring  the  publication  as  its  official  organ,  and  notes.  Since 
the  majority  of  titles  are  published  in  P’yCngyang,  place  of  publication 
is  given  only  for  titles  issued  in  other  cities. 

Information  on  the  holdings  of  the  five  libraries  covered 
is  given  as  of  December  31,  1970.

the  East  Asiatic  Library  of  the  University  of  California,  Berkeley), 
are  furnished  immediately  following  their  symbols:  DLC,  NNC,  MH-HY 
and  WaU;  and  the  availability  of  some  of  the  materials  on  microfilm 
is  indicated  at  the  end  of  the  information  on  their  holdings. 

Finally,  I  acknowledge  my  indebtedness  to  the  Social  Science 
Research  Council  which  provided  me  with  financial  aid  to  complete  this 
list.  I  would  also  like  to  acknowledge  the  support  and  encouragement 
given  me  by  Dr.  Warren  Tsuneishi  and  Dr.  Edwin  G.  Beal,  Chief  and 
Assistant  Chief  of  the  Library’s  Orientalia  Division;  and  by  Dr.  Gari  K. 
Ledyard  (Chairman)  and  members  of  the  Joint  Committee  on  Korean 
Studies,  SSRC  and  ACLS . 

My  thanks  are  due  also  to  Miss  Yoon  Whan  Choi  and  Mr.  Karl  Lo 
of  the  Far  Eastern  Library,  the  University  of  Washington,  Seattle; 


Messrs.  Sungha  Kim  and  Eugene  Wu  of  the  Harvard-Yenching  Library; 
Messrs.  Yong  Kyu  Cnoo  and  Raymond  Tang  of  the  East  Asiatic  Library 
of  the  University  of  California,  Berkeley;  and  Mr.  Hyung-Suk  Chai 
of  the  East  Asian  Library,  Columbia  University,  for  the  assistance 
they  have  rendered  in  gathering  data.
