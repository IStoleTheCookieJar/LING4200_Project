EXPLANATION  APPLICABLE  TO  ALL  TABLES 


THIS  PUBLICATION  IS  BASED  ON  REPORTS  SUBMITTED  BY  PRIVATE  EXPORTERS.  OUTSTAND¬ 
ING  EXPORT  SALES  AS  REPORTED  AND  COMPILED  WITH  OTHER  DATA  GIVE  A  SNAPSHOT  VIEW 
OF  THE  CURRENT  CONTRACTING  SCENE.  AT  ANY  GIVEN  TIME  IN  THE  COURSE  OF  A  MARKET¬ 
ING  YEAR,  OUTSTANDING  SALES  DO  NOT  BEAR  A  CONSISTENT  RELATIONSHIP  TO  EVENTUAL 
EXPORT  SHIPMENTS.  A  MEANINGFUL  EXPORT  PROJECTION  IS  NOT  OBTAINABLE  BY  THE 
SIMPLE  DEVICE  OF  ADDING  OUTSTANDING  SALES  TO  EXPORTS  TO  DATE.  THE  LATTER  DATA, 
ALONE  PROVIDE  A  MORE  RELIABLE  MEASURE  OF  CURRENT  EXPORT  ACTIVITY  THAN  MAY 
BE  DERIVED  FROM  A  YEAR-TO-YEAR  COMPARISON  OF  OUTSTANDING  SALES. 


ALL  COUNTRIES  WITH  OUTSTANDING  SALES  OR  ACCUMULATED  EXPORTS  ARE  INCLUDED  FOR 
EACH  CLASS  OF  WHEAT,  ALL  WHEAT,  WHEAT  PRODUCTS,  CORN,  SOYBEANS,  SOYBEAN  CAKE 
AND  MEAL,  AMERICAN  PIMA  COTTON,  ALL  UPLAND  COTTON  AND  CATTLE  HIDES  AND  SKINS. 

FOR  OTHER  COMMODITIES,  COUNTRIES  WITH  LESS  THAN  1%  OF  TOTAL  OUTSTANDING  SALES  OR 
ACCUMULATED  EXPORTS  ARE  NOT  SHOWN  SEPARATELY  BUT  ARE  INCLUDED  IN  REGION  TOTALS.

Export  Sales  Highlights 


This  summary  is  based  on  reports  from  exporters  for  the  period  May  8-14,  1992. 

NOTE:  The  acronym  "USSR"  refers  to  the  republics  of  the  former  Soviet  Union. 

Wheat :  Minor  activity  produced  net  sales  of  38,700  MT  as  small  increases  for  the  USSR  (51,100  MT ) ,  Japan 

(  20,000  MT),  and  unknown  destinations  (15,000  MT )  were  partially  offset  by  decreases  for  Yemen  (58,800 
MT).  Sales  of  164,500  MT  for  the  1992/93  marketing  year  (MY)  were  mainly  to  Japan  (64,000  MT ) ,  Israel 
(40,000  MT),  and  South  Korea  (28,000  MT )  .  Exports  slowed  to  345,900  MT — down  40  percent  from  the  prior 
week  and  48  percent  from  the  4-week  average.  The  principal  destinations  were  South  Korea  (65,500  MT ) , 
the  USSR  (53,300  MT )  ,  Algeria  (50,400  MT ) ,  and  Yemen  (47,300  MT ) . 

Corn :  Sales  of  710,300  MT  advanced  16  percent  from  the  prior  week  and  the  4-week  average.  The  major 

buyers  were  Japan  (233,600  MT )  ,  South  Africa  (210,000  MT ) ,  and  Mexico  (115,300  MT )  .  Exports  of  507,000 
MT  were  off  42  percent  from  the  week  earlier  and  38  percent  from  the  4-week  average.  Japan  (207,600  MT), 
Taiwan  (141,300  MT )  ,  South  Korea  (55,000  MT ) ,  and  Algeria  (51,300  MT )  were  the  primary  recipients. 

Barley :  Sales  of  2,600  MT — all  to  the  USSR — were  down  42  percent  from  the  week  earlier  and  91  percent 
below  the  4-week  average.  Exports  of  72,200  MT  were  one-third  below  the  previous  week,  but  6  percent 
above  the  4-week  average.  The  primary  destinations  were  the  USSR  (52,300  MT )  and  Israel  (18,200). 

Sorghum :  Net  sales  of  12,800  MT  resulted  as  increases  for  Mexico  (32,500  MT )  and  Japan  (  25,300  MT)  were 
partially  offset  by  decreases  for  unknown  destinations  (45,000  MT ) .  Exports  of  220,100  MT  were  one-third 
below  the  prior  week  and  14  percent  under  the  4-week  average.  Mexico  (197,100  MT)  was  the  major 
destination . 

Rice :  Sales  of  13,800  MT  were  down  7  percent  from  the  week  earlier  and  59  percent  from  the  4-week 

average.  Mexico  (8,400  MT — all  rough),  Saudi  Arabia  (6,400  MT )  ,  the  Netherlands  (3,500),  and  Haiti 
(2,900  MT )  were  the  primary  buyers.  Decreases  were  reported  for  Belgium  (4,400  MT )  and  Canada  (3,500 
MT ) .  Exports  of  76,100  MT  were  six  and  one-fifth  times  the  prior  week  and  two  and  one-tenth  times  the 
4-week  average.  The  primary  destinations  were  Turkey  (39,000  MT )  and  Costa  Rica  (16,700  MT ) . 

Soybeans :  Net  sales  of  294,400  MT  were  four  and  one-third  times  the  prior  week's  marketing-year  low  and 

more  than  double  the  4-week  average.  Major  increases  for  Japan  (159,100  MT )  ,  unknown  destinations 
(87,000  MT),  and  Israel  (43,900  MT )  were  partially  offset  by  decreases  for  Mexico  (34,200  MT )  .  Exports 
of  63,200  MT  were  off  70  percent  from  the  previous  week  and  three-quarters  lower  than  the  4-week  average. 
Mexico  (37,100  MT)  was  the  principal  destination,  accounting  for  59  percent  of  the  week's  exports. 

Soybean  Cake  and  Meal:  Net  sales  of  29,600  MT  were  six  times  the  previous  week's  low  level,  but  fell  by 
one-half  from  the  4-week  average.  Increases  for  Venezuela  (19,600  MT),  Canada  (7,200  MT ) ,  and  Colombia 
(7,000  MT )  were  partially  offset  by  decreases  of  15,000  MT  for  unknown  destinations.  Exports  of  74,500 
MT  were  down  one-half  from  the  week  earlier  and  one-third  from  the  4-week  average.  The  principal 
destinations  were  the  USSR  (23,600  MT )  ,  Algeria  (15,800  MT ) ,  Venezuela  (10,200  MT )  ,  Israel  (9,600  MT ) , 
and  Canada  (8,700  MT ) . 

Soybean  Oil:  Sales  of  6,400  MT  were  off  88  percent  from  the  previous  week's  marketing-year  high  and  61 
percent  less  than  the  4-week  average.  The  major  buyers  were  Algeria  (3,000  MT),  Morocco  (2,300  MT ) ,  and 
Canada  (700  MT ) .  Sales  of  5,000  MT  for  MY  92/93  were  to  Morocco.  Exports  of  400  MT  were  mainly  to 
Canada . 

Cotton :  Sales  for  the  current  marketing  year  of  21,100  running  bales  (RB)  were  one-half  below  the  prior 

week's  level  and  one-fifth  under  the  4-week  average.  The  major  buyers  were  Japan  (6,300  RB),  Guatemala 
(4,400  RB),  and  Taiwan  (3,700  RB )  .  Sales  of  205,100  RB  for  delivery  in  MY  1992/93  resulted  primarily 
from  increases  of  189,800  RB  for  unknown  destinations.  Exports  of  183,300  RB  were  31  percent  above  the 
week  earlier  and  the  4-week  average.  Asian  destinations  accounted  for  59  percent  of  the  week's 
shipments;  Africa,  23  percent;  West  European,  12  percent;  Western  Hemisphere,  5  percent,  and  East 
European,  1  percent. 

Hides  and  Skins:  Sales  of  513,100  pieces  were  39  percent  higher  than  the  preceding  week's  level  and  57 
percent  above  the  4-week  average.  Whole  cattle  hide  sales  of  468,300  pieces  were  primarily  for  Japan 
(197,200  pieces)  and  South  Korea  (165,100  pieces).  Exports  of  420,600  pieces  were  28  percent  above  the 
previous  week  and  30  percent  over  the  4-week  average.  Whole  cattle  hide  shipments  of  368,100  pieces  were 
destined  mainly  for  South  Korea  (151,100  pieces)  and  Japan  (112,300  pieces). 

Sales  of  29,700  wet  blue  hides  were  54  percent  below  the  week  earlier  and  14  percent  under  the  4-week 
average.  South  Korea  (12,500  unsplit),  Indonesia  (5,500  unsplit),  and  Japan  (5,300  unsplit)  were  the 
major  buyers  of  wet  blue  hides.  Exports  of  46,200  hides  were  86  percent  above  the  previous  week  and  53 
percent  over  the  4-week  average.  The  primary  destinations  were  South  Korea  (14,400  unsplit),  Italy 
(10,900  unsplit),  Japan  (6,200  grain  split  and  3,800  unsplit),  and  Taiwan  (8,900  unsplit).  Sales  of 
splits  totaled  476,100  pounds — up  19  percent  from  the  week  earlier,  but  down  67  percent  from  the  4-week 
average.  South  Korea  (374,600  pounds)  was  the  leading  buyer,  followed  by  Spain  (48,700  pounds)  and  China 
(46,000  pounds).  Exports  of  1,412,700  pounds  were  three-quarters  above  the  prior  week  and  91  percent 
over  the  4-week  average.  The  primary  recipients  were  South  Korea  (374,600  pounds),  Mexico  (320,000 
pounds),  Thailand  (230,500  pounds),  and  Hong  Kong  (202,800  pounds).
